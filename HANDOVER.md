2. **PR12 — H-TTL-1 compares `candidate_outcomes.replay_r` on BOTH sides, not `trade_outcomes.realized_r` for the approved arm.** The spec's own framing suggested "expired proposals' counterfactual replay_r vs. approved-and-executed proposals' realized_r," which would need a `trade_proposals → positions → trade_outcomes` join chain (schema-verified: `trade_outcomes` is keyed by `position_id`, not `proposal_id`). 3. **PR12 — the resolver never auto-sets MET/FAILED/WITHDRAWN, only proposed → testing → resolved.** PORT-1's generic `rejected`/`forward-test-candidate`/`inconclusive` verdict vocabulary describes which side of zero a CI lands on — it does NOT by itself say whether that confirms or refutes a given hypothesis's own directional claim. The 8 seeded hypotheses mix positive-claimed (H-TQS-1/H-CAT-1/H-INT-1: `forward-test-candidate`=confirmed), negative-claimed (H-POL-1: `rejected`=confirmed), either-direction-informative (H-WIN-1/H-TTL-1: only `inconclusive` is bad news), and inverted-good-news (H-REJ-1: a `rejected` verdict is the CONFIRMING case for operator judgment, not a failure). Deferred, not rejected — revisit once real evaluated data exists and it's clear a human is repeatedly doing this translation by hand.
